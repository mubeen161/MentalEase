Stress is Dangerous

Physiological Effects: Chronic stress triggers the body's "fight or flight" response, releasing stress hormones such as cortisol and adrenaline. These hormones are useful in short-term situations, but prolonged exposure to them can lead to increased heart rate, elevated blood pressure, and a weakened immune system. Over time, this can contribute to the development of cardiovascular diseases, hypertension, and a compromised ability to fight off infections

Mental Health Disorders: Prolonged stress is a significant risk factor for the development of mental health disorders such as anxiety and depression. It can alter brain chemistry and impact areas of the brain responsible for regulating mood, leading to persistent negative emotions and emotional instability.
